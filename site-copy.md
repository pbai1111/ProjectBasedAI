# Theater & Immersive Arts

We built VirtualArtsTV on the conviction that audiences don't just want to watch — they want to be an active part of the community.

### Clients — Artists & Cultural Partners

Alicia Keys · Amanda Palmer · Puffs Off-Broadway/Fathom Events · Bedlam Theater Company · The Flea Theater · TRUTH The Musical · The Lark Play Development Center · The New York International Fringe Festival · Patriot Brass Ensemble · Associated Chamber Music Players · SteeleDance · Dance New Amsterdam · Exit 12 Dance Company · SleepDance · Major League Hackers

### Story

In 2007, I asked a question almost no one in the arts was asking: what happens when live performance meets the internet — not as a recording, but as a real-time, interactive, global experience?

I spent the next decade answering it. I founded VirtualArtsTV — the first company dedicated to live-streaming the performing arts to audiences worldwide while integrating social media into the real-time experience. We produced the first live-streamed digital play. We launched the first ever live-streamed performing arts festival. We built educational programs. We shot and directed live theatrical broadcasts for national distribution.

Years before the pandemic forced every theater company onto Zoom, we had already built the model, tested the technology, and proven the audience was there.

# Live Interactive Production

By building gaming engagement software into professional multi-camera nonprofit productions, we turned passive audiences into dedicated communities. They raised millions.

### Selected Clients

Biden Victory Fund · Muscular Dystrophy Association · The Women's March · ERA Coalition · AFL-CIO · Victory Fund · Family Equality · Vote With Us · March for our Lives · Brick Education

### Story

We turned virtual fundraisers into broadcast-quality, multi-camera productions — complete with world-class event design, custom motion graphics, and gaming-style engagement mechanics that made audiences feel like participants, not spectators.

The innovation wasn't putting fundraisers on a gaming platform like Twitch — although we built those too, for MDA. It was the reverse: bringing real-time leaderboards, live thermometers, gamified giving, and integrated donation mechanics into professional productions on Zoom, YouTube, and Facebook.

And after every event, we collected and analyzed the data — what drove donations, when audiences engaged, where they dropped off — so that every next fundraiser raised more than the last.

The work for Family Equality, The Biden Victory Fund, Brick Education, and dozens of national nonprofits all proved the same principle: a transactional ask becomes something entirely different when you design it as a communal, emotional experience.
